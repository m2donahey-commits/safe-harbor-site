üí¨ Understanding Trauma

Healing takes time, and your reactions are normal responses to trauma. You are not alone.

  • Learn about common trauma responses like anxiety, fear, or numbness.
  • Understand how trauma-informed care supports recovery.
  • Practice grounding and self-soothing techniques.

üíô What to Expect When Seeking Help

Knowing what happens next can make reaching out feel less overwhelming.

  • Advocates listen without judgment and support your choices.
  • You decide what information to share.
  • Services are confidential unless someone is in immediate danger.
  • Learn what to expect at shelters, hospitals, or counseling appointments.

üå± Rebuilding Your Life

Rebuilding takes time and happens in small steps. These resources are here to support your financial stability, safety, and emotional healing at your pace.

Financial Recovery

  • Local financial assistance and emergency aid programs.
  • Safe banking resources for survivors (separate accounts, privacy tips).
  • Budgeting templates and free financial coaching services.
  • Information about grants, benefits, and public assistance.

Employment & Career Support

  • Job training and certification programs designed for survivors.
  • Free or low-cost resume and interview coaching.
  • Workforce re-entry programs with flexible or trauma-informed workplaces.
  • Tools to identify your strengths, skills, and interests.

Housing Stability

  • Emergency, transitional, and long-term housing options.
  • Rental assistance and housing vouchers where available.
  • Tenant rights information for survivors.
  • Safety planning for choosing and securing a new home.

Health & Emotional Well-Being

  • Directories of trauma-informed therapists and support groups.
  • Low-cost or sliding-scale counseling and mental health services.
  • Self-care and grounding practices you can do at home.
  • Ideas for building a simple, sustainable wellness routine.

Building Support Networks

  • Peer support groups for survivors (online and in-person).
  • Mentorship or buddy programs, when available.
  • Community centers, healing circles, and wellness workshops.
  • Tips for setting boundaries and protecting your peace.

Goal Setting & Planning

  • Short- and long-term goal examples for safety, work, and home.
  • Break-it-down planning: turning big goals into small, realistic steps.
  • Ideas for tracking progress and celebrating wins.
  • A reminder that it‚Äôs okay to adjust your plan as life changes.

You do not have to work on all of these areas at once. Choosing even one small next step is a powerful act of courage.
